# Position and Approach of Worshipping Sekhmet regarding ISO/IEC 42001 – Decision Basis

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** 16 June 2026  
**Classification:** Public (with possible internal addenda)  
**Status:** Draft for internal decision

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines Worshipping Sekhmet’s position and strategic approach toward ISO/IEC 42001:2023 (Artificial Intelligence Management Systems). It serves as an internal decision basis while also providing transparency and guidance for external parties that may wish to align their activities with Worshipping Sekhmet principles.

---

## 2. Context of ISO/IEC 42001

ISO/IEC 42001 is a relatively new international standard (published in 2023) that specifies requirements for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving an Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS). While it introduces useful concepts such as AI risk management, impact assessment, and human oversight, its practical maturity — especially in high-stakes, high-autonomy environments — remains limited.

Worshipping Sekhmet acknowledges the existence of ISO 42001 but does not consider it a primary or leading normative reference at this stage.

---

## 3. Alignment with Worshipping Sekhmet Principles

Worshipping Sekhmet’s core governance is defined by **WS-ViC-001** (Immutable Core Principles) and the **WS-Framework-Charter**. Many requirements addressed in ISO 42001 are already covered — often more rigorously — by these foundational documents, in particular:

- Meaningful Human Oversight (MHO)
- Principle of Truth, Justice, Order, Balance, and Responsible Care
- Gateway Control (information and decision flows from higher to lower trust layers)
- Traceability and Accountability
- Need-to-Know / Right-to-Know

Where ISO 42001 requirements align with these principles, they may be used as supplementary administrative or documentation tools. However, they do **not** replace or override WS core principles.

---

## 4. Critical Assessment from an Orbital Reality Perspective

In environments involving high-autonomy systems — particularly those operating in orbital or other safety-critical domains — decisions can have irreversible physical, ethical, and strategic consequences.

From this perspective, ISO 42001 shows several limitations:

- It places significant emphasis on stakeholder perception and organisational objectives rather than on objective truth and long-term consequences.
- The standard’s risk management approach does not sufficiently prioritise irreversible harm and the protection of human dignity in high-autonomy contexts.
- Requirements for human oversight remain relatively generic and do not yet reflect the complexity of meaningful oversight in systems where human intervention windows may be extremely short or non-existent.
- The standard currently lacks robust mechanisms to ensure that AI systems remain subordinate to higher-order ethical and governance frameworks.

For these reasons, Worshipping Sekhmet treats ISO 42001 as a **supporting reference** rather than a normative foundation.

---

## 5. Worshipping Sekhmet Position

Worshipping Sekhmet’s position regarding ISO/IEC 42001 is as follows:

- **Internal use:** ISO 42001 is not adopted as a primary management system standard. Core governance, risk management, and oversight requirements are defined by WS-ViC-001 and the WS-Framework-Charter.
- **External engagement:** Worshipping Sekhmet may recognise ISO 42001 compliance efforts by partner organisations, provided they do not conflict with WS principles.
- **Use of VAS ADT tools:** The VAS ADT Companion Template (including its structured compliance sections) may be used by external parties as a supporting instrument when mapping their ISO 42001 implementation to WS-aligned governance.

---

## 6. Guidance for External Parties

Organisations that are required or choose to implement ISO/IEC 42001 and wish to align with Worshipping Sekhmet principles are advised to:

1. Use **WS-ViC-001** and the **WS-Framework-Charter** as the primary reference framework.
2. Treat ISO 42001 requirements as supplementary administrative controls.
3. Ensure that any ISO 42001-derived processes (e.g. impact assessments, risk registers) are explicitly validated against WS principles, particularly Meaningful Human Oversight, Truth, and Responsible Care.
4. Use the **VAS ADT Companion Template** – specifically **Annex F (ISO 42001 Mapping & Application Guide)** – as a structured method to document and verify this alignment. This annex provides a ready-to-use, clause-by-clause mapping to WS principles, enabling efficient gap analyses and audit preparation without compromising the core WS framework.

---

## 7. Conclusion and Next Steps

Worshipping Sekhmet maintains a cautious but open stance toward ISO/IEC 42001. The standard may offer useful supporting practices, but it does not replace the foundational principles defined in WS-ViC-001 and the WS-Framework-Charter.

This position will be reviewed periodically as ISO 42001 matures and as practical experience in high-autonomy and orbital environments increases.
