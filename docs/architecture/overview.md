# Architecture Overview

**Worshipping Sekhmet – Foundational Stabilization Framework**  
*Version 2.1 | 15 June 2026*

## 1. Introduction

**Worshipping Sekhmet (WS)** is the foundational stabilization framework for fully vetted Valkyries. It establishes the doctrinal, ethical, and operational principles that govern the exercise of Valkyrie authority.

The **Global Security Framework** is operated by Valkyries **as a service** within Worshipping Sekhmet, dedicated to the upholding and realization of Ma’at. This architecture describes how WS principles are implemented through organizational structures, technical systems, and governance processes across seven sovereign pillars, each led by a Valkyrie in Control.

**WS-ViC-001** remains the highest normative document at the policy level. Trust is primarily established through system accreditations in accordance with the **Valkyrie Accreditation Scheme (VAS)**. Hybrid Merkle-Root Anchoring and decentralized verifiability serve as technical mechanisms to support the integrity, auditability, and long-term verifiability of accredited systems.

Meaningful Human Oversight (MHO) is a strategic concept of central importance in complex and multi-actor environments. Within Worshipping Sekhmet, it is now being operationalized through organizational structures, processes, and technical systems across the seven sovereign pillars.

MHO exists on two levels:

- **Individual level**: Valkyries must possess the necessary certification and competence to exercise effective oversight.
- **System and process level**: Systems and processes must be designed in such a way that meaningful human oversight can be exercised effectively — both structurally and procedurally.

The **Valkyrie Accreditation Authority (VAA-ViC)** accredits the implementation of MHO within systems and processes. Operational responsibility for exercising MHO generally lies with the respective Valkyries. However, where a sovereign actor (such as a state) maintains a sufficient level of Meaningful Human Oversight, operational responsibility may be exercised by that actor instead.

## 2. Strategic Objective

The primary objective of the framework is the establishment and maintenance of **trust** as a foundation for stability and long-term cooperation. Trust is primarily achieved through systematic accreditations according to the Valkyrie Accreditation Scheme (VAS). Technical mechanisms such as hybrid Merkle-Root Anchoring and decentralized verifiability support the integrity and auditability of accredited systems and processes.

## 6. Technical Architecture – Hybrid Anchoring

To ensure long-term integrity and decentralized verifiability, the framework employs a **hybrid Merkle-Root Anchoring** approach:

- Critical documents, decisions, and system states are hashed.
- Merkle Trees are used to efficiently aggregate multiple hashes.
- Merkle Roots are periodically anchored on a tamper-evident ledger.
- This enables verifiable integrity without exposing sensitive content.

This technical layer supports the trust established through VAS accreditations and enables Strategic Mutual Trust (SMT) with external partners.

## 7. Governance and Accountability

- **Valkyrie in Command**: Holds strategic oversight and ultimate authority over the entire framework.
- **Valkyries in Control (ViC)**: Each sovereign pillar is led by a Valkyrie in Control, who is responsible for strategic leadership, operational governance, and Meaningful Human Oversight within their respective pillar.

In case of conflict, the order of precedence is:

1. **WS-ViC-001** (Immutable Core Principles) – highest normative document
2. **WS-Framework-Charter**
3. Operational and pillar-specific documents (e.g. VAS)
